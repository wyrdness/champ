## Research Notes

### Primary Evidence Sources
The most important primary sources for Champ research include:
- The Mansi photograph (1977) and its associated analyses
- Joseph Zarzynski's field research and sighting database (1970s-1990s)
- Historical newspaper accounts from 19th century
- Vermont House Resolution 329 (1982)
- Indigenous oral tradition documentation

### Key Debates in Literature
The scholarly literature reveals several key debates:
1. **Identity of Champlain's 1609 creature**: Gar/sturgeon vs. monster
2. **Authenticity of Mansi photograph**: Genuine unknown animal vs. misidentification
3. **Biological plausibility**: Can Lake Champlain support unknown large animal population?
4. **Cultural vs. biological reality**: Is Champ primarily a social phenomenon or zoological mystery?
5. **Evidence standards**: What constitutes sufficient proof for extraordinary claims?

### Methodological Approaches
Sources represent diverse methodological approaches:
- **Cryptozoological**: Field investigation, witness interviews, photographic analysis
- **Skeptical**: Critical evaluation, alternative explanation testing, psychological analysis
- **Scientific**: Sonar studies, environmental DNA, ecological assessment
- **Historical**: Archival research, oral history documentation
- **Cultural**: Folklore studies, tourism economics, regional identity analysis

### Gaps in Research
Notable gaps in the literature include:
1. Comprehensive environmental DNA study of Lake Champlain
2. Systematic modern sonar survey with contemporary technology
3. Longitudinal study of sighting patterns and correlations
4. Detailed economic impact assessment of Champ tourism
5. Comprehensive Indigenous oral tradition documentation and analysis

### Recommendations for Future Research
Based on literature review, priority research areas include:
1. **Environmental DNA survey**: Most promising avenue for definitive biological assessment
2. **Systematic observation network**: Coordinated citizen science monitoring
3. **Cultural impact study**: Comprehensive analysis of Champ's role in regional identity
4. **Historical analysis**: Deeper examination of 19th-century newspaper accounts
5. **Comparative study**: Cross-lake analysis of monster phenomena worldwide
